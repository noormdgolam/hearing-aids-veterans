Veterans Guide to Sensorineural Hearing Aids in Alaska

Sensorineural hearing loss is one of the most common hearing impairments affecting military veterans. As a veteran living in Alaska, you may be eligible for compensation and assistance through the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs (VA). This guide aims to provide an overview of sensorineural hearing loss, the process of obtaining a hearing aid through the VA, and resources available to veterans in Alaska.

What is Sensorineural Hearing Loss?

Sensorineural hearing loss is a type of permanent hearing impairment that occurs when there is damage to the inner ear, or the auditory nerve. This can be caused by exposure to loud noises, such as gunfire, explosions, or machinery, as well as other factors like age, genetics, or certain medications. Sensorineural hearing loss can lead to difficulties communicating with family and friends, experiencing isolation, and affecting overall quality of life.

Veterans Eligibility for Hearing Aids

If you are a veteran and have been diagnosed with sensorineural hearing loss, you may be eligible for hearing aids through the VA. The VA offers hearing aids, evaluations, and other related services to eligible veterans as part of their benefit package. To be eligible, you must meet the following criteria:

  • Have a compensable, service-connected disability rating for a hearing-related condition.
  • Be diagnosed with a hearing loss condition that is determined to be permanent in nature.

To initiate the process, you can submit an application for VA disability compensation, which includes completing a Form 21-526EZ, Veteran's Application for Compensation and/or Pension. If your hearing loss is service-related, you may be entitled to compensation and can receive hearing aids through the VA.

How to Obtain a Hearing Aid through the VA

If you have been approved for a hearing aid through the VA, you can obtain it through the following steps:

  1. Contact the VA Medical Center in Anchorage or Fairbanks to schedule an audiology evaluation.
  2. Attend a comprehensive audiological evaluation to assess the severity of your hearing loss.
  3. Meet with a VA audiologist to discuss hearing aid options and make a selection.
  4. The hearing aid will be ordered, and you will be scheduled for a follow-up appointment to adjust the aid and provide further instruction.

Additionally, you can contact the Alaska Commission on Aging for information on resources and services available for hearing aid assistance for seniors.

Resources for Veterans in Alaska

There are several resources available to veterans living in Alaska who experience hearing loss or hearing-related difficulties:

  • VA Medical Center, Anchorage: (907) 257-4700
  • VA Medical Center, Fairbanks: (907) 474-7900
  • Alaskan Commission on Aging: (907) 269-6861
  • Alaska Veterans Affairs: (907) 271-4301

This guide provides an overview of the process of obtaining a hearing aid through the VA as a veteran living in Alaska. For more information and to initiate the process, contact the VA Medical Center in Anchorage or Fairbanks, or visit the VA website at va.gov.
