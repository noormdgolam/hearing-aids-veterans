Veterans Guide to Sensorineural Hearing Aids in Arkansas

Published: July 15, 2026 • Written by Editorial Team

As a veteran living in Arkansas, you may be entitled to various benefits and services that can help you navigate the complex world of healthcare. If you're struggling with hearing loss due to age, injury, or service conditions, understanding your options for sensorineural hearing aids is crucial.

Sensorineural hearing loss is the most common form of permanent hearing impairment, accounting for up to 90% of all cases. This type of hearing loss affects the inner ear or the auditory nerve, which can make it difficult to hear sounds, especially in noisy environments or when communicating with people who have softer speech. Sensorineural hearing loss can be caused by various factors, including noise-induced hearing loss, age-related hearing loss, and certain medications.

Veterans with sensorineural hearing loss may be eligible for a variety of benefits through the Department of Veterans Affairs (VA). The VA offers hearing aids to qualifying veterans, either through its own facilities or by reimbursing the cost of private hearing aids. To be eligible, you must have a service-connected disability rating of at least 10% and have hearing loss that is a direct result of your military service.

When selecting a sensorineural hearing aid, it's essential to consider your specific needs and preferences. Hearing aids come in different types, including behind-the-ear (BTE) models, in-the-ear (ITE) models, and completely-in-canal (CIC) models. BTE models are more visible but offer more amplification, while ITE models are smaller but may provide less amplification. CIC models are the smallest, most discreet option but may not be suitable for all individuals.

In addition to the type of hearing aid, you'll also need to consider factors such as power level, style, and features. Some hearing aids come with specialized features, such as noise reduction, feedback suppression, and direct audio input (DAI) capabilities. When selecting a hearing aid, it's crucial to work closely with a licensed audiologist or hearing specialist who can assess your specific needs and recommend the most suitable device.

In Arkansas, there are several reputable audiologists and hearing specialists who can help you find the right sensorineural hearing aid. You can start by contacting your local VA medical center or the VA Regional Office to inquire about available resources and referrals. Additionally, consider consulting with non-VA providers who specialize in hearing disorders, such as the University of Arkansas for Medical Sciences or the Arkansas Hearing and Speech Center.

Financing and reimbursement options are also available for veterans seeking sensorineural hearing aids. The VA offers a hearing aid allowance, which enables eligible veterans to purchase hearing aids with minimal out-of-pocket costs. Some private insurance providers may also offer coverage for hearing aids, depending on your individual plan. Be sure to carefully review your policy to understand your coverage options and any associated costs.

Finally, don't hesitate to ask for help navigating the complex process of selecting a sensorineural hearing aid. As a veteran, you've already made countless sacrifices for your country. By taking advantage of available resources and benefits, you can improve your quality of life and regain your connection to the world around you.
