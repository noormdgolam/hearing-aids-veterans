Veterans Guide to Conductive Hearing Aids in Vermont

Published: July 15, 2026 • Written by Editorial Team

Conductive hearing aids, also known as behind-the-ear (BTE) hearing aids, may be a suitable option for veterans in Vermont who experience conductive hearing loss due to middle ear issues such as otosclerosis, perforated eardrums, or earwax buildup.

According to the Vermont Department of Veterans Affairs, veterans can consult with a licensed audiologist or hearing specialist at the White River Junction VA Medical Center's Audiology Clinic to assess their hearing and determine the best course of treatment.

What are Conductive Hearing Aids?

Conductive hearing aids are specifically designed to bypass the affected middle ear and amplify sound directly to the eardrum. These devices are typically larger than other types of hearing aids and usually consist of a microphone, amplifier, and receiver that convert sound waves into electrical signals, which are then transmitted to the eardrum via a tube.

Conductive hearing aids work by utilizing a tympanostomy tube to bypass the middle ear and directly stimulate the eardrum, thereby restoring hearing in individuals with conductive hearing loss.

The different types of conductive hearing aids available include:

  • Power BTE hearing aids
  • Mini BTE hearing aids
  • Titanium BTE hearing aids
  • Custom-fit BTE hearing aids

Veterans Benefits and Financing Options

The VA offers several benefits and financing options for veterans in Vermont to receive conductive hearing aids, including:

  • VA compensation for hearing loss
  • Veterans' choice program
  • VA hearing aid funding
  • Discount programs for veterans

The White River Junction VA Medical Center's Audiology Clinic offers a comprehensive audiology service, including hearing assessments, hearing aid fittings, and follow-up appointments to ensure optimal hearing outcomes.

Finding an Audiologist in Vermont

Veterans in Vermont can search for licensed audiologists in their area by visiting the Vermont Department of Health's website or contacting the White River Junction VA Medical Center's Audiology Clinic for a referral.

It is essential to work with a licensed audiologist who has experience in fitting conductive hearing aids and to follow their recommendations for regular maintenance and adjustments to optimize hearing outcomes.

Veterans seeking conductive hearing aids should consult with a licensed audiologist at the White River Junction VA Medical Center's Audiology Clinic to discuss their individual needs and determine the best course of treatment.
