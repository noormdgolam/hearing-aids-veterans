A Comprehensive Guide to Sensorineural Hearing Aids in Idaho

Sensorineural hearing loss is a common condition affecting thousands of veterans in Idaho. It is characterized by damage to the inner ear or the nerve pathway leading to the brain, resulting in impaired hearing or complete loss of hearing in one or both ears. For veterans with sensorineural hearing loss, sensorineural hearing aids can be an effective solution to restore their ability to hear and communicate effectively.

Types of Sensorineural Hearing Aids:

  • Behind-the-Ear (BTE) hearing aids: These are one of the most common types of hearing aids and work by amplifying sound that reaches the eardrum. The power source and processor are housed in a case that fits behind the ear, and a tube connects the case to an earpiece that fits inside the ear.
  • Receiver-in-Canal (RIC) hearing aids: Similar to BTE hearing aids, RIC hearing aids are smaller and more discreet than BTE hearing aids. They work by amplifying sound that reaches the eardrum, and the receiver is placed inside the ear canal.
  • Canal Hearing Aids (CIC): These are the smallest and most discreet type of hearing aid, completely inserted into the ear canal.
  • Bone Anchored Hearing Aids (BAHA): These are designed for individuals with single-sided deafness or those who have a conductive hearing loss in one ear. They work by bypassing the external ear and amplifying sound directly through the skull.

Selection Criteria for Sensorineural Hearing Aids:

  • Severity of hearing loss: Different types of hearing aids are suitable for individuals with various levels of hearing loss.
  • Age: Newer hearing aid technologies are more suitable for younger individuals, while older models may be more suitable for older individuals.
  • Activity level: Individuals with a high activity level may prefer hearing aids that are more durable and have a longer battery life.
  • Personal preferences: Some individuals may prefer hearing aids that are more discreet, while others may prefer hearing aids with more features.

Accessing Sensorineural Hearing Aids in Idaho:

The VA in Idaho provides comprehensive hearing services, including hearing aids, to eligible veterans. To access these services, veterans can contact the Boise VA Medical Center or the local VA outpatient clinics in Idaho. Additionally, veterans can also purchase hearing aids from licensed audiologists or hearing aid specialists.

Pricing and Cost Assistance:

The cost of sensorineural hearing aids can vary widely, depending on the type, brand, and features of the device. Veterans may be eligible for cost assistance through the VA's Audiology and Speech Pathology Services, including free hearing aids for those with a 100-percent disability rating. It is essential to discuss the cost and cost assistance options with a VA audiologist or a licensed audiologist.

Tips for Choosing the Right Sensorineural Hearing Aids:

  • Consult with a VA audiologist or a licensed audiologist to determine the most suitable type and brand of hearing aid.
  • Try before you buy: Many audiologists and hearing aid specialists offer free trials or demo periods to enable individuals to try out different hearing aids.
  • Consider the warranty and aftercare services offered by the manufacturer.
  • Read reviews and ask for referrals from friends or family members who have used the same hearing aid.
