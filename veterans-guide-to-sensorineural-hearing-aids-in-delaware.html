Veterans Guide to Sensorineural Hearing Aids in Delaware

Published: July 15, 2026 • Written by Editorial Team

If you're a veteran experiencing hearing difficulties due to sensorineural hearing loss, you may be eligible for compensation and assistance from the Department of Veterans Affairs (VA). In Delaware, veterans can access specialized services and resources to help them navigate the process of obtaining a hearing aid.

Sensorineural hearing loss occurs when there's damage to the inner ear or the nerves that transmit sound to the brain. It's the type of hearing loss most often associated with aging, but it can also be caused by exposure to loud noises, certain medications, or head injuries. For veterans, the noise-induced hearing loss (NIHL) from military service can be a contributing factor.

The VA recognizes sensorineural hearing loss as a service-connected disability and provides various levels of compensation based on the extent of the individual's impairment. To be eligible for compensation, veterans must provide medical evidence indicating a service connection between their hearing loss and military service.

In Delaware, the Wilmington VA Medical Center offers comprehensive audiology services, including hearing evaluations, audiograms, and device fittings. The VA also partners with local audiologists and hearing specialists who are trained to work with veterans and their unique needs.

Veterans who qualify for a hearing aid through the VA can choose from various models and styles, including behind-the-ear (BTE), in-the-ear (ITE), and completely-in-canal (CIC) devices. The VA covers the cost of the hearing aid, as well as the costs associated with its maintenance and upkeep.

To obtain a hearing aid through the VA, veterans must first undergo a comprehensive audiology evaluation to ensure the device is properly fitted and tailored to their specific needs. This evaluation may involve a hearing test, as well as discussions with a VA audiologist about the type of hearing aid suitable for the individual's lifestyle and preferences.

The VA also provides guidance on the various programs and benefits available to veterans with hearing impairments. For example, the Vocational Rehabilitation and Employment (VR&E) program helps eligible veterans prepare for and maintain employment despite their service-connected disabilities.

Additionally, the Delaware Division for the Visually Impaired and the Department of Education offer resources and services for veterans with visual impairments and hearing impairments. These services may include assistive technology training, counseling, and adaptive equipment.

For those seeking assistance in accessing these services and benefits, the Veterans Service Organizations (VSOs) in Delaware are a valuable resource. Organizations such as the American Legion, VFW, and Disabled American Veterans offer guidance and support to veterans navigating the VA system.

In summary, veterans with sensorineural hearing loss in Delaware have access to a range of resources and services designed to support them throughout the process of obtaining a hearing aid. By understanding the eligibility requirements and benefits available, veterans can take the first step toward reclaiming their hearing and improving their quality of life.
