Veterans Guide to Conductive Hearing Aids in Missouri

Missouri, a state rich in history and natural beauty, also boasts of an extensive network of medical facilities and audiology clinics catering to the needs of its residents. What are Conductive Hearing Aids?

Conductive hearing aids are medical devices specifically designed to treat conductive hearing loss, a condition where sound is blocked from reaching the inner ear due to problems in the middle or external ear. This type of hearing loss is often caused by issues such as eustachian tube dysfunction, otosclerosis, or perforated eardrums. Conductive hearing aids can be customized to suit individual needs, using various technologies and materials to amplify sound and improve hearing clarity.

Eligibility and Access for Veterans

As a veteran, you may be eligible for financial assistance, priority access, or other benefits in receiving conductive hearing aids in Missouri. To determine your eligibility, start by contacting the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs (VA) and requesting an audiology evaluation at a VA medical facility. If you are deemed eligible, you may be prescribed a conductive hearing aid, which can be fitted and adjusted by a certified audiologist or hearing specialist within the VA system.

Non-VA Options for Veterans in Missouri

If you choose to seek non-VA care for conductive hearing aids, there are numerous audiology clinics and hearing centers in Missouri that offer a range of hearing solutions. Some examples include:

  • The Hearing Center at Missouri Baptist Medical Center in St. Louis, which offers comprehensive audiological services and specializes in conductive hearing loss treatment.
  • Ear, Nose and Throat Associates of the Ozarks in Springfield, which has a dedicated audiology department and uses cutting-edge technology to diagnose and treat conductive hearing loss.
  • Audiology Associates of Kansas City, which provides personalized hearing solutions, including conductive hearing aids, using the latest advancements in audiology.

Financial Assistance and Resources

As a veteran, you may be eligible for various financial assistance programs to help cover the cost of conductive hearing aids. These include:

  • The VA's Hearing Aid Benefit Program, which provides financial assistance to eligible veterans for hearing aids and audiological services.
  • The Missouri Department of Social Services' Medicaid program, which covers audiological services, including conductive hearing aid fittings, for low-income individuals, including veterans.
