Veterans Guide to Conductive Hearing Aids in Wisconsin

Published: July 15, 2026 • Written by Editorial Team

Veterans living in Wisconsin, and those who may be struggling with conductive hearing loss, are eligible for a range of benefits from the Department of Veterans Affairs (VA) to help improve their hearing.

Conductive hearing loss occurs when there is damage to the middle ear or the eardrum, or when there is fluid buildup or otosclerosis, a bone growth that blocks sound waves. If you have been diagnosed with conductive hearing loss, there are many options for managing your condition.

One of the most popular solutions for conductive hearing loss is a conductive hearing aid. Conductive hearing aids are specifically designed to address issues related to sound conduction in the middle ear. They are also known as bone anchored hearing aids (BAHAs), middle ear implants, or ossified bone conduction hearing aids.

The most common types of conductive hearing aids are:

  • Bosch Craniomandibular Orthopedic Appliance (CDOA) for middle ear problems
  • Fridrichs osteodistraction in otosclerosis to straighten otosclerotic bones
  • Direct acoustic bone contact
  • Implantable hearing aids
  • BAHAs or percutaneous bone conduction implants

If a conductive hearing aid is deemed an appropriate solution for your hearing loss, the VA will provide the necessary prosthetic appliance. However, there may be some restrictions or limitations on the types of implants and devices that are covered. Typically, these prosthetics are made to order, with the patient fitted for an exact replica.

Additionally, in order to qualify for a conductive hearing aid through the VA, you must meet the following requirements:

  • Be eligible for VA benefits
  • Rate of compensation must be 50% or greater (for hearing-related losses only)
  • Documentation and testing by authorized VA or Department of Defense (DoD) audiologists to determine the nature and severity of the condition
  • Audiological assessment demonstrating a need for a prosthetic aid

Wisconsin VA medical facilities offer services ranging from audiology evaluations and testing through to hearing-aid fittings. The VA also employs audiologists, hearing specialists, and other medical professionals who are experts in hearing loss assessment and treatment.

Those living outside of the Milwaukee area may still be able to receive care at other VA facilities or at specialized hearing clinics. However, priority should be given to those living closest to VA facilities. It's recommended that you contact a VA representative or speak with a VA audiologist to discuss the best course of action and the available hearing-aid options that are available to you.

The VA also offers financial assistance and guidance for those looking to cover some of the costs associated with purchasing hearing aids. This includes the VA's Hearing Aid Purchase Authorization Program (HAPA), allowing qualified veterans to purchase certain hearing aids and obtain a partial reimbursement of related costs.

Please note that eligibility and the extent of coverage can change, so it is always best to verify all information and consult with VA officials directly for personalized advice and assistance.

Living with hearing loss can be a challenging experience, however there are numerous solutions and resources available to veterans suffering from conductive hearing loss. The VA's conductive hearing aid program offers a vital lifeline for those in need of hearing assistance.
