Sensorineural Hearing Aids in Alabama: A Guide for Veterans

A sensorineural hearing loss is a common condition that affects many veterans. It occurs when there is damage to the inner ear or the auditory nerve, which can be caused by exposure to loud noises, age, or certain medical conditions. In this guide, we will discuss the causes of sensorineural hearing loss, the types of hearing aids available, and how to obtain them in Alabama.

The symptoms of sensorineural hearing loss can vary from person to person. Some common symptoms include:

  • Ringing or buzzing in the ears
  • Difficulty hearing soft sounds
  • Muffled speech or sounds
  • Difficulty understanding speech in noisy environments
  • Headaches or ear pain

There are several causes of sensorineural hearing loss, including:

  • Loud noises, such as explosions or gunfire, can cause damage to the hair cells in the inner ear
  • Aging can cause natural wear and tear on the hair cells in the inner ear
  • Medical conditions, such as Meniere's disease or otosclerosis, can cause damage to the inner ear
  • Exposure to certain medications or toxins, such as ototoxic antibiotics or noise-inducing chemicals
  • Genetic disorders, such as Usher syndrome or Pendred syndrome

Fortunately, there are several types of hearing aids available that can help to amplify sound and improve communication. The following are some common types of hearing aids:

  • Behind-the-ear (BTE) hearing aids, which are small electronic devices that sit behind the ear and are connected to an earpiece in the ear canal
  • Completely-in-canal (CIC) hearing aids, which are small electronic devices that are inserted deep into the ear canal and are nearly invisible
  • Invisible-in-canal (IIC) hearing aids, which are smaller than CIC hearing aids and sit just beyond the second bend of the ear canal
  • Receiver-in-canal (RIC) hearing aids, which are similar to BTE hearing aids but have a receiver that is inserted into the ear canal
  • Bone-anchored hearing aids (BAHAs), which use a surgical implant to transmit sound through the skull to the inner ear

In Alabama, veterans can obtain sensorineural hearing aids through the following:

  • The Department of Veterans Affairs (VA) offers hearing aids to veterans who have a hearing loss of 40 decibels or greater in one or both ears
  • The VA also offers assistive listening devices, such as hearing assistive technology (HAT) or communication access real-time translation (CART)
  • The Alabama Department of Veterans Affairs also offers resources and support for veterans with hearing loss
  • Private audiologists and hearing aid specialists in Alabama can also prescribe and fit hearing aids for veterans

The cost of hearing aids can vary depending on the type of device and the individual's needs. However, in some cases, the VA may cover the cost of hearing aids or provide financial assistance to help veterans obtain the devices they need. Veterans who are interested in obtaining hearing aids in Alabama should contact the VA or a private audiologist to discuss their options and determine the best course of action.
