Veterans Guide to Conductive Hearing Aids in New Hampshire

Published: July 15, 2026 • Written by Editorial Team

For veterans in New Hampshire, addressing conductive hearing loss can be a vital step in maintaining their overall health and quality of life. A conductive hearing loss condition occurs when sound is unable to reach the inner ear due to a problem in the outer or middle ear, such as a blockage by earwax, a middle ear infection, or a punctured eardrum.

A hearing aid may be prescribed to treat conductive hearing loss, helping to mitigate the impact of the condition on everyday interactions and relationships.

VA-Approved Providers in New Hampshire

In New Hampshire, veterans can access hearing aid services from providers approved by the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs (VA). These providers offer comprehensive audiological evaluations, hearing aid fitting, and ongoing support to ensure the best possible outcomes. A partial list of VA-approved providers in New Hampshire includes:

  1. Veterans Administration White River Junction Medical Center (Lebanon)
  2. The Dartmouth-Hitchcock Audiology Clinic (Lebanon)
  3. Hadley Memorial Rehabilitation Department (Manchester)

Veterans are advised to contact these providers directly to inquire about their specific hearing aid services and eligibility criteria.

Financial Assistance for Veterans

Veterans with a service-connected disability rating of 10% or higher may be eligible for VA financial assistance to cover the costs of hearing aids and related services. The VA may reimburse veterans for certain expenses, including:

  • Conductive hearing loss evaluation and treatment
  • Custom-made hearing aids
  • Vision therapy or orientation and mobility training

Veterans can apply for reimbursement through the VA's online claims system or by contacting their local VA office for guidance.

Treatment Options

VA-approved providers in New Hampshire may offer a variety of conductive hearing aids to suit individual needs and preferences. Treatments may include:

  • Battery-powered hearing aids
  • Rechargeable hearing aids
  • Behind-the-ear (BTE) hearing aids
  • In-the-ear (ITE) hearing aids

The costs of hearing aids and related services can vary depending on factors such as the type of aid, materials used, and the provider's fees. Veterans may want to shop around to compare costs and services before making a decision.

A basic, analog hearing aid may cost in the range of $500-$1,500, while a more advanced, digital hearing aid can range from $1,500 to $4,000 or more.

Next Steps

Veterans seeking conductive hearing aid services in New Hampshire should schedule a consultation with a VA-approved provider to discuss their specific needs and develop a personalized plan of care. To prepare for your appointment, be sure to:

  • Ask about your eligibility for VA financial assistance
  • Discuss your lifestyle, interests, and communication goals
  • Share any existing medical records or test results

Additional Resources

Veterans with questions or concerns about conductive hearing loss or hearing aid services may find additional support through the following resources:

  • U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs: (800) 827-1000 or va.gov
  • New Hampshire Department of Health & Human Services: (603) 271-9444 or nh.gov/dhhs
  • The National Institute on Deafness and Other Communication Disorders: (800) 241-1044 or nidcd.nih.gov
