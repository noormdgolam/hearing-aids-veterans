Veterans Guide to Conductive Hearing Aids in Oklahoma

Published: July 15, 2026 • Written by Editorial Team

As a veteran living in Oklahoma, you may be eligible for benefits to assist with hearing loss treatment, including conductive hearing aids. Conductive hearing aids are designed to amplify sound before it reaches the eardrum, providing an effective solution for individuals with conductive hearing loss.

Conductive hearing loss occurs when there is a problem with the middle ear's ability to conduct sound waves. This type of hearing loss can be caused by a variety of factors, including infections, trauma, and congenital conditions such as otosclerosis.

The benefits of conductive hearing aids for veterans in Oklahoma are numerous. These devices can greatly improve an individual's ability to communicate and engage with their environment, and may also have a positive impact on overall quality of life.

To be eligible for benefits for conductive hearing aids through the Department of Veterans Affairs (VA), veterans must meet certain criteria. This includes:

  • Diagnosis of conductive hearing loss by a licensed healthcare professional
  • A minimum level of disability, as determined by a VA audiology evaluation
  • Availability of VA benefits, including those through the Montgomery GI Bill or Vocational Rehabilitation and Employment (VR&E) program

The process of obtaining conductive hearing aids through the VA typically involves the following steps:

  1. Scheduling a VA audiology appointment to assess hearing loss and determine eligibility for benefits
  2. Receiving a prescription for conductive hearing aids, which must be filled at a VA-approved audiologist or hearing specialist
  3. Paying for the hearing aids either out-of-pocket or through VA benefits, depending on eligibility

When selecting a hearing aid, veterans should consider the following factors:

  • Style: Behind-the-ear (BTE) or in-the-ear (ITE) devices both offer effective sound amplification, but may differ in terms of style and fit
  • Power: Higher power levels may be required for more severe hearing loss
  • Features: Some devices may offer additional features, such as Bluetooth connectivity or tinnitus relief

Some popular conductive hearing aid models include:

  • BTE: Oticon Opn S; Phonak Sky Q
  • ITE: Bernafon Inteo; Unitron Moxi Fit

It is essential for veterans to consult with a VA-approved audiologist or hearing specialist to determine the best course of treatment for their specific hearing loss needs.

Veterans can access resources and benefits by contacting the following organizations:

  • VA Hearing Loss and Tinnitus Services: (877) 222-VETS (8387)
  • VA Disability Compensation: (800) 827-1000
  • American Speech-Language-Hearing Association (ASHA) - Oklahoma: (405) 841-4646
