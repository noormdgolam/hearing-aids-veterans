Veterans Guide to Sensorineural Hearing Aids in New Hampshire

Published: July 15, 2026 • Written by Editorial Team

Sensorineural hearing loss is a condition that affects approximately 28 million people in the United States, with a significant portion residing in New Hampshire.

This progressive condition occurs when there is damage to the inner ear or the nerve pathways that carry sound to the brain, leading to a decrease in sound clarity and sensitivity.

For veterans, sensorineural hearing loss is often the result of military service, particularly if they served in combat zones with high levels of noise exposure.

The good news is that advances in technology have made hearing aids an effective solution for managing sensorineural hearing loss, and New Hampshire has numerous options for veterans seeking the best aid possible.

Hearing aids for sensorineural hearing loss have come a long way since their inception, with modern devices offering improved sound quality, reduced battery maintenance, and enhanced ease of use.

When shopping for a hearing aid, veterans should consider the following factors:

  • Severity of hearing loss: Veterans with severe hearing loss may require more powerful hearing aids, while those with mild hearing loss may benefit from more discreet options.
  • Frequency range: Some hearing aids focus on specific frequency ranges, such as high-frequencies for music or low-frequencies for conversation.
  • Tone and resonance: Veterans may prefer hearing aids that enhance the natural tone and resonance of their voice, making conversations more comfortable.
  • Rechargeability: Rechargeable hearing aids require less maintenance and can save veterans money on battery replacements.
  • Discreetness: Some veterans prefer more discreet hearing aids, such as Invisible-in-the-Canal (IIC) or Completely-in-Canal (CIC) models.

New Hampshire has a number of resources for veterans seeking hearing aid assistance:

  • Veterans Affairs (VA) hospitals and clinics: Veterans can schedule an appointment with an audiologist or otolaryngologist to assess their hearing and receive a hearing aid evaluation.
  • Hearing aid manufacturers: Companies like Starkey, Phonak, and Oticon offer hearing aids specifically designed for veterans, with programs and discounts available through the VA.
  • Third-party audiological services: Private audiologists and hearing specialists may offer more flexible payment plans and personalized care.

When selecting a hearing aid provider, veterans should ensure the following:

  • Veterans' benefits coverage: The provider should be familiar with VA benefits and be able to help veterans navigate the process.
  • Personalized fit and adjustment: A thorough fit and adjustment process is crucial to ensure optimal hearing aid performance.
  • Rehabilitation and support: Providers should offer ongoing support and education to help veterans adjust to their new hearing aids.

New Hampshire resources include:

  • Veterans Affairs Medical Center, White River Junction, Vermont (serves New Hampshire)
  • Otolaryngology and Audiology at Elliot Hospital, Manchester, NH
  • Pediatric Audiology and Ear, Nose, and Throat (ENT) Center, Lebanon, NH

Additionally, veterans can find more information on the following websites:

  • Veterans.gov
  • Health.mil
  • PBSound.com for information on hearing aids

It's crucial for veterans to stay informed about their hearing health and seek assistance from qualified professionals for the best outcome.

Sensorineural hearing loss may have a significant impact on a veteran's quality of life, but modern hearing aids can restore communication confidence and improve overall well-being.

To get started with finding a hearing aid solution in New Hampshire, veterans can:

  • Contact the local VA hospital or clinic to schedule an appointment with an audiologist or otolaryngologist.
  • Visit the website of local hearing aid manufacturers or third-party audiological services to learn more about their programs and pricing.
  • Reach out to organizations such as the Starkey Hearing Foundation or the Phonak Hearing Foundation for additional information and support.

Remember to always prioritize the expertise and knowledge of audiologists or hearing specialists to ensure a proper fit and adjustment of hearing aids.
