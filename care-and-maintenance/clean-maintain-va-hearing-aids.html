How to Clean and Maintain Your VA Issued Hearing Aids

By Dr. Sarah Mitchell, Audiologist & Veterans Advocate Updated: 2026-02-09

How to Clean and Maintain Your VA-Issued Hearing Aids

The hearing aids provided by the Department of Veterans Affairs (VA) are designed to help veterans improve their hearing and communication. However, proper cleaning and maintenance are essential to ensure the longevity and effectiveness of these devices.

Understanding Your Hearing Aid Components

  • Dry the outer casing and ear tips regularly with a soft cloth to prevent damage from moisture.
  • Use the cleaning brush provided with your hearing aid to remove debris from the earpiece and other openings.

Recommended Cleaning Methods

Scheduled maintenance will ensure your VA-issued hearing aid continues to function properly.

  1. Daily Cleaning: Wipe the device with a soft cloth and water-free cleaning solution to remove dirt and oil from the skin.
  2. Weekly Deep Cleaning: Use the cleaning brush to remove debris from the earpiece, and use a soft-bristled toothbrush to gently scrub the outer casing.
  3. Monthly Battery Check: Replace the battery as indicated by the manufacturer, as old batteries may affect hearing aid performance.

VA-Recommended Maintenance

The VA offers regular scheduled maintenance for your hearing aid, ensuring that any necessary adjustments or repairs are made in a timely manner.

  • Schedule a maintenance appointment every 6 months with a VA audiologist or a hearing aid specialist to check for any issues.
  • Attend any recommended follow-up appointments to ensure optimal hearing aid performance.

How to Store Your Hearing Aid

When not in use, store your hearing aid safely to prevent damage and prolong its life.

  • Use the carrying case provided with your hearing aid to store the device when not in use.
  • Keep the case in a clean, dry location, away from children and pets.
  • Never store your hearing aid in direct sunlight or in extremely hot or cold temperatures.

Reporting Issues or Damage

Timely reporting of any issues or damage to your hearing aid is crucial to ensure timely repairs and continued effective use.

  • Contact the VA to report any loss, damage, or malfunction of your hearing aid.
  • Follow the correct procedures for submitting a claim for replacement or repair.

Additional Resources

The VA offers a variety of resources to help veterans with hearing loss and their caregivers.

  • VA website: [https://www.va.gov](https://www.va.gov) - Learn more about VA benefits and hearing aid services.
  • HEAR (Hearing Examination and Reporting) Program: Contact your local VA medical facility to learn more about this program.

Proper maintenance and regular check-ups with a VA audiologist or hearing aid specialist will help ensure that your VA-issued hearing aid continues to function at optimal levels.
