How to Dry Wet Hearing Aids Safely

By Dr. Sarah Mitchell, Audiologist & Veterans Advocate Updated: 2026-04-30

Safe Storage and Maintenance of Hearing Aids: A Guide for Veterans

Hearing aids are a vital aid for individuals with hearing loss, and for veterans, they can be a lifeline to reconnect with their community and improve overall quality of life. However, proper care and maintenance of these devices are crucial to ensure their longevity. When not in use, wet hearing aids can be a breeding ground for bacteria and moisture damage. In this article, we will discuss the importance of drying wet hearing aids safely, the risks associated with improper drying, and provide step-by-step instructions to help veterans properly store and care for their hearing aids.

The Risks of Improper Drying: Moisture Damage and Infection

  • Maintenance of Acoustic Properties - Moisture accumulation can lead to a significant reduction in sound quality, affecting the overall performance of the hearing aid.
  • Auditory Damage - Excessive moisture can cause damage to the internal components of the hearing aid, leading to irreparable harm.
  • Bacterial Growth - Moisture-logged hearing aids can foster the development of bacteria, which poses serious health risks to users.
  • Short Service Life - Inadequate maintenance can lead to shortened service life for hearing aids.

Safe Drying Methods:

Several drying methods can be employed to preserve the integrity of the hearing aid while avoiding damage or exposure to moisture. The following methods are recognized as the most effective for ensuring the longevity of hearing aids:

  1. Desiccant Sachets - Utilizes dry silica gel packets to absorb excess moisture. Ensure the sachet is designed specifically for hearing aids and follow the manufacturer's instructions.
  2. Reactivating Dryers - These units are designed to gently remove moisture from hearing aids. Reactivating dryers can be found online for purchase or loaned through organizations supporting veterans with hearing loss.
  3. Air Drying - Place the hearing aid upside down on paper to dry slowly, with a minimum distance from surfaces that may come into contact with dust, water, or any objects that could fall and hit the hearing aid.

Additional Tips for Longevity

  • Regular Cleaning - Utilize a soft cloth and mild soap solution to maintain cleanliness and remove debris.
  • Store Hearing Aids Separately - Store each hearing aid in its own case, to avoid cross-contamination and minimize exposure to environmental factors that could cause damage.
  • Regular Maintenance Visits - Schedule regular professional cleanings and check-ups with an audiologist or hearing specialist to diagnose and correct potential issues before they become severe problems.
