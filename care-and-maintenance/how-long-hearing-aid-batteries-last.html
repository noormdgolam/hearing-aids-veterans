How Long Do Hearing Aid Batteries Last?

By Dr. Sarah Mitchell, Audiologist & Veterans Advocate Updated: 2026-03-06
X f in

Understanding Hearing Aid Battery Life for Vets

Caring for a hearing loss condition can be challenging, especially for veterans who often have been exposed to loud noises in military environments. One crucial aspect to consider is the hearing aid batteries, which power your hearing device and can impact its effectiveness. In this article, we'll delve into the factors influencing hearing aid battery life and what you can expect from different types of batteries.

Factors Affecting Battery Life:

  • Tone Control: The frequency range of your music and conversations can affect battery life.
  • Volume: Higher volumes consume more power, reducing battery life.
  • Battery Type: Zinc-air, mercury-free, and silver oxide batteries have varying lifespans.
  • Usage Patterns: Continuous use, rather than regular on/off operation, can reduce battery life.

Types of Hearing Aid Batteries:

There are four primary types of hearing aid batteries:

  • National Institute for Occupational Safety and Health (NIOSH) Certified Batteries: The most common type, these batteries offer a balance between power and lifespan.
  • Zinc-Air Batteries: Ideal for low-power devices, these batteries have a longer shelf life but shorter usage period.
  • Zinc-Carbon Batteries: Cheaper and less powerful than zinc-air batteries, these should be avoided.
  • Silver Oxide Batteries: High-powered and long-lived, but also more expensive.

How Long Do Hearing Aid Batteries Last?

The lifespan of hearing aid batteries varies depending on the type:

  • National Institute for Occupational Safety and Health (NIOSH) Certified Batteries: 250-300 hours
  • Zinc-Air Batteries: 1-2 weeks (80-140 hours)
  • Zinc-Carbon Batteries: 2-5 days (20-60 hours)
  • Silver Oxide Batteries: 3-6 months (1,500-3,000 hours)

VA Benefits for Hearing Aids:

The Department of Veterans Affairs (VA) offers various benefits for veterans with hearing loss, including:

  • Free hearing aids for low-income veterans.
  • Priority access to audiology and hearing aid services.
  • Presumptive service connection for hearing loss conditions.
  • Annual audiological evaluations and hearing aid maintenance.

To learn more about the VA benefits for hearing aids, we recommend contacting the VA directly or visiting their website. A thorough understanding of hearing aid battery life and available VA benefits can empower you to make informed decisions about your hearing care.
