State Assistance for Veterans Hearing Aids in California

By Dr. Sarah Mitchell, Audiologist & Veterans Advocate Updated: 2026-08-28

State Assistance for Veterans Hearing Aids in California

The state of California offers various assistance programs and benefits for veterans seeking hearing aids. These programs can help alleviate the financial burden associated with hearing aid purchases and related services.

One of the primary resources available to veterans in California is the California Department of Veterans Affairs (CDVA). This department provides support and services specifically designed for veterans, including access to healthcare and benefits. Veterans can contact the CDVA to inquire about hearing aid assistance and other related benefits.

California Veteran Assistance Programs

  • The California Department of Rehabilitation's (DOR) Hearing Aids for the Disabled (HAD) Program provides financial assistance for hearing aids and related services to eligible individuals, including veterans.
  • The California Department of Social Services (CDSS) offers the California Alternative Payment Program (CAPP), which provides financial assistance for hearing aids and related services to eligible low-income individuals, including veterans.
  • The California Association of Health Care Interpreters (CAHCI) and the National Association of the Deaf (NAD) offer hearing aid assistance and education programs for veterans and their families.

VA Benefits for Hearing Aids

The U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs (VA) offers several benefits that can assist veterans with hearing aid costs, including:

  • The Veterans Service Organizations (VSO) - Veterans can seek assistance from accredited VSOs, such as the Disabled American Veterans (DAV) and the American Legion, to navigate the process of applying for VA benefits, including hearing aid assistance.
  • The VA's Audiological and Speech Pathology Services: The VA provides audiological services, including hearing aid evaluations and fittings, at VA medical centers and outpatient clinics.
  • The VA's Hearing Aids Program: Eligible veterans may be eligible for a free or reduced-price hearing aid through the VA's Hearing Aids Program.
  • The VA's Assistive Listening Devices (ALDs) Program: Eligible veterans may be eligible for an ALD, which is a portable device that connects to a home hearing aid, television, or phone to enhance sound quality.

Eligibility for State and VA Benefits

To be eligible for state assistance and VA benefits, veterans must meet specific requirements, including:

Veterans must have documentation of hearing loss or hearing impairment from a VA medical facility or a private audiologist.

Veterans must have a valid California driver's license or ID card.

Veterans must apply for benefits through the VA's Health Benefits Program and meet the medical requirements for hearing aid assistance.

Apply for Benefits

To apply for state assistance and VA benefits for hearing aids, veterans can follow these steps:

Contact the CDVA to inquire about local assistance programs.

Apply for benefits through the VA's Health Benefits Program, either online or by mail.

Seek assistance from an accredited VSO to navigate the application process.

Visit the California Department of Rehabilitation's (CDOR) website for information on the Hearing Aids for the Disabled (HAD) Program.

Conclusion

State assistance for veterans hearing aids in California can provide financial relief and support for those in need. Eligible veterans can apply for benefits through the CDVA, the VA, and private organizations offering hearing aid assistance programs. By understanding the eligibility requirements and applying for the correct benefits, veterans can access hearing aids and related services to improve their quality of life.
