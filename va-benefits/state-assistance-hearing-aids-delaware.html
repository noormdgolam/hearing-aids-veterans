State Assistance for Veterans Hearing Aids in Delaware

In Delaware, eligible veterans can receive state assistance for hearing aids, in addition to benefits provided by the Department of Veterans Affairs (VA). The Delaware Division of Veterans Services serves as a liaison between state and federal governments to provide veterans with access to these benefits.

Eligibility Criteria

  • Must be a veteran or service-connected disabled veteran
  • Must have a documented hearing impairment
  • May not have private insurance or adequate financial resources to cover hearing aid costs

The Delaware VA Hospital and clinics serve as referral points for state assistance. Applicants are encouraged to consult with audiology staff at one of these facilities to determine eligibility and facilitate the application process.

Applying for State Assistance

To receive assistance, veterans must submit an application with the following supporting documentation:

  • DD Form 214 (Member Copy 4)
  • Documentation of hearing impairment (e.g., audiogram results)
  • Completed application for state assistance (available at state veterans facilities or on-line)

Once the application is submitted, it will be reviewed by the Delaware Division of Veterans Services. If approved, the applicant will be referred to a state-approved hearing aid provider to begin the evaluation and fitting process.

Available Benefits

State assistance for hearing aids in Delaware includes:

  • Hearing aids purchased through state-approved providers at no cost to the veteran
  • Coverage for hearing aid batteries, maintenance, and adjustments
  • Access to audiological services and evaluation for hearing impairments
  • Support and orientation to ensure veterans are comfortable using their new hearing aids

VA Benefits for Hearing Aids

The VA may also provide hearing aids to eligible veterans, either through direct purchase or reimbursement for costs associated with hearing aid purchase. Veterans must first seek an audiological evaluation from a VA Medical Center or clinic.

  1. Consult with a VA audiologist to determine eligibility and receive a copy of the VA's hearing aid authorization form (VAF 10-1274)
  2. Choose a state-approved hearing aid provider or private provider who has contracted with the VA
  3. Have the hearing aids purchased and fitted by the provider
  4. In most cases, the VA will reimburse or purchase the hearing aids, depending on the veteran's status as a service-connected or nonservice-connected disabled veteran

Contact the Delaware VA Hospital or a nearby VA clinic for guidance on the application process and specific requirements for VA benefits. For state assistance, veterans can contact the Delaware Division of Veterans Services for more information.

Delaware veterans who are experiencing hearing loss are encouraged to reach out to both the VA and the Delaware Division of Veterans Services for assistance and guidance. These resources can help connect veterans with the hearing aids they need and facilitate access to state and federal benefits.
