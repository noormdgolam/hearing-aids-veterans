Veterans Guide to Conductive Hearing Aids in Hawaii

Published: July 15, 2026 • Written by Editorial Team

Conductive hearing aids, a crucial device for individuals with conductive hearing loss, can significantly improve one's ability to communicate in their daily lives. Conductive hearing loss occurs when there is a problem with the middle ear's ability to conduct sound. This type of hearing loss can be caused by a variety of factors, including earwax buildup, otosclerosis, or a perforated eardrum.

When it comes to selecting a conductive hearing aid, it's essential to find a professional audiologist or hearing specialist who is experienced in fitting these types of devices. In Hawaii, the Department of Veterans Affairs (VA) offers exceptional services for veterans, including audiology services and hearing aids.

Under the VA's audiology services, eligible veterans can receive a free hearing and ear exam, as well as a hearing aid evaluation. If a hearing aid is deemed necessary, the VA will provide the prescribed device. However, it's essential to note that the VA's hearing aids program has some limitations and waiting periods, and not all types of hearing aids may be covered.

For veterans who prefer to use a private audiologist or opt for a more advanced hearing aid, there are several reputable providers in Hawaii who offer conductive hearing aids. Some popular options include:

  • Med-El Hearing Solutions: Known for their high-quality hearing aids and cutting-edge technology, Med-El offers a wide range of conductive hearing aids tailored to meet individual needs.
  • Starkey Hearing Technologies: With years of experience in developing innovative hearing solutions, Starkey offers a variety of conductive hearing aids that can be customized to fit specific hearing requirements.
  • Oticon Hearing Solutions: With a focus on evidence-based hearing health solutions, Oticon provides high-quality conductive hearing aids designed to deliver crystal-clear sound.

In addition to choosing the right provider, it's essential to consider the type of conductive hearing aid that is best suited for your needs. Some options include:

  • Behind-the-ear hearing aids (BTEs): These devices consist of a microphone, amplifier, and receiver housed in a small casing that sits behind the ear.
  • In-the-ear hearing aids (ITEs): These devices are custom-fit and sit inside the ear canal or shell of the ear.
  • Completely-in-canal hearing aids (CICs): These devices are custom-fit and inserted directly into the ear canal.

Before selecting a conductive hearing aid, it's crucial to consult with a professional audiologist or hearing specialist to determine the best device for your specific needs and budget. With the right device, you can significantly enhance your ability to communicate and enjoy the beauty and tranquility that Hawaii has to offer.

When seeking audiology services or hearing aids, the VA offers the following benefits for eligible veterans:

  • Free hearing and ear exams
  • Hearing aid evaluation and fitting
  • Coverage for certain types of hearing aids
  • Access to a network of VA-approved providers

It's essential to note that specific benefits may vary depending on individual circumstances and eligibility. Contact the VA or consult with a professional audiologist or hearing specialist for more information on available benefits.

To learn more about the VA's audiology services and hearing aids program, or to obtain assistance with claims, please call the following numbers:

  • VA Hearing Aid and Ear Service Hotline: 1-800-686-7688
  • VA Benefits and Services: 1-800-827-1000

Additionally, please visit the VA's website for more information on services and benefits available to eligible veterans:

  • VA Website: https://www.va.gov
