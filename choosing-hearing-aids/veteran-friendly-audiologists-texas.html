Finding Veteran-Friendly Audiologists in Texas

By Dr. Sarah Mitchell, Audiologist & Veterans Advocate Updated: 2028-03-25
X f in

Find Reliable Audiologists for Your Veterans Administration (VA) Benefits in Texas

For veterans with hearing impairments, seeking help from a trusted and experienced audiologist is crucial. Choosing a veteran-friendly audiologist in Texas can ensure that you receive the VA benefits you are entitled to, including timely and efficient services. The U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs (VA) provides eligible veterans access to audiology services for the assessment and treatment of hearing impairments related to military service.

VA Audiological Services in Texas

  1. Eligible veterans may receive audiological services, including hearing aids and other hearing devices, at VA facilities across the state.
  2. VA providers use approved devices that meet the Federal Standards for the Assessment and Treatment of Hearing Impairments.
  3. Eligible veterans may also receive audiological services through the VA's Community Care Program.

Choosing a Veteran-Friendly Audiologist in Texas

  1. Ask your primary care provider for recommendations.
  2. Check online reviews and ratings on trusted sources such as Yelp or Google.
  3. Check with the American Speech-Language-Hearing Association (ASHA) or the American Academy of Audiology (AAA) for certifications and qualifications.
  4. Confirm the audiologist has experience working with veterans and their specific needs.

Texas VA Audiology Offices and Facilities

  • VA Central Texas Health Care System - Temple
  • North Texas Veterans Health Care System - Dallas
  • Michael E. DeBakey VA Medical Center - Houston

Military Hearing Loss Compensation

The VA offers disability compensation for hearing loss linked to military service, including:

  • Permanent loss of one ear (service-connected)
  • Loss of one ear due to surgery or injury (not service-connected)
  • Reduced hearing capacity

Requesting Hearing Aid Compensation

Eligible veterans may request hearing aid compensation through their local VA facility by:

  1. Contact your primary care provider for referral to audiology services.
  2. Follow-up testing and assessments to diagnose hearing impairments linked to military service.
  3. Apply for VA benefits online, in person, or through mail.
  4. Follow-up on your claim after submitting it.

For more information about audiology services in Texas, veterans may contact the Central Texas VA regional office or visit the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs online at va.gov. To find a provider who participates in the VA's Community Care Program, visit the VA's website.
