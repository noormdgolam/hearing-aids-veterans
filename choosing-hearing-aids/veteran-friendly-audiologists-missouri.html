Finding Veteran-Friendly Audiologists in Missouri

By Dr. Sarah Mitchell, Audiologist & Veterans Advocate Updated: 2027-06-29
X f in

Find a Veteran-Friendly Audiologist in Missouri

If you're a veteran in Missouri seeking quality hearing care and guidance on utilizing your VA benefits, you're not alone. With numerous audiologists statewide, choosing the right one for your needs can be daunting. To begin this process, consider the following steps to locate a veteran-friendly audiologist in Missouri.

The United States Department of Veterans Affairs (VA) encourages veterans to seek private hearing healthcare services when possible. These options can provide a more timely and convenient experience compared to waiting for an appointment at a VA facility. Additionally, a private audiologist may be able to offer more comprehensive treatment plans and access to a wider range of hearing technologies.

Start by asking your primary care physician for recommendations. They may be familiar with local audiologists who have experience working with veterans and can provide the care you need. Additionally, ask friends, family members, or fellow veterans for suggestions.

  • Check the VA website: The VA provides a comprehensive database of certified audiologists with experience working with patients with service-connected hearing loss. To find a list of licensed audiologists in Missouri, visit the VA website's Hearing Aids section and click on the link that reads 'Find a VA-Certified Audiologist.'
  • Check professional associations: Organizations like the American Speech-Language-Hearing Association (ASHA) and the American Academy of Audiology (AAA) have directories that help find certified audiologists in your area. Visit their websites and search for audiologists in Missouri to get a list of options.
  • Verify credentials and experience: Make sure the audiologist you choose is licensed to practice in the state of Missouri and has experience working with patients with service-connected hearing loss. You can also check if they have any specialized certifications, such as the Certified Audiologist (CA) or the Fellow of the American Academy of Audiology (FAAA).

Once you have a list of potential audiologists, you should consider the following factors when making your decision:

  • Expertise: Look for audiologists with experience working with patients with hearing loss due to service in the military.
  • Communication style: Choose an audiologist with a communication style that meets your needs.
  • Treatment options: Consider audiologists who can provide a range of hearing treatment options, including hearing aids, cochlear implants, and balance and vestibular care.

Many audiologists offer free consultations, which can help you gauge your comfort level with their bedside manner and determine if they are the right fit for your needs. Some audiologists may also have online reviews or ratings that can give you an idea of their quality of care and patient satisfaction.

Remember to ask your audiologist about your potential eligibility for VA benefits and the process for submitting claims. A knowledgeable audiologist can guide you through this process and help you get the compensation you deserve.

With the right audiologist by your side, you can regain control over your hearing health and enjoy the benefits of clear and confident communication. By taking the time to research and choose a veteran-friendly audiologist in Missouri, you'll be well on your way to finding the care you need and the respect you deserve.

To find a veteran-friendly audiologist near you, begin by following these steps: Research and make a list of potential audiologists, contact them to schedule a consultation, and verify their credentials and experience. By doing so, you'll be able to find the right audiologist in Missouri to meet your unique needs and provide the hearing care you deserve.
