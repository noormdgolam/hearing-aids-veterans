Finding Veteran-Friendly Audiologists in Connecticut

By Dr. Sarah Mitchell, Audiologist & Veterans Advocate Updated: 2026-10-02
X f in

The men and women who have served in our country's armed forces sacrifice a great deal, including their hearing. According to the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs (VA), about 60% of the service members returning from Iraq and Afghanistan suffer from tinnitus and an estimated 2.7 million Veterans suffer from some degree of hearing loss.

When searching for an audiologist, it's crucial to find someone who understands and caters to the specific needs of veterans. A veteran-friendly audiologist can ensure that you receive the necessary care and access to VA benefits, such as reduced or eliminated fees for hearing aids.

  • Knowledge of VA benefits and processes: A veteran-friendly audiologist should be well-versed in the VA's audiology care and benefits, including claims procedures and paperwork.
  • Accredited by the VA: Many veteran-friendly audiologists are accredited by the VA, ensuring they meet specific standards for quality care.
  • Experience working with veterans: A veteran-friendly audiologist should have experience working with veterans, understanding their unique needs, and tailoring a treatment plan accordingly.

If you're a veteran in Connecticut seeking a qualified audiologist, here are some steps you can follow:

  • Contact the VA Connecticut Healthcare System: Reach out to the VA Connecticut Healthcare System or a local VA hospital for recommendations on accredited audiologists in your area.
  • Visit the VA's website: Use the VA's website to search for audiologists who are certified by the National Board of Certification in Audiometry (NBC-A) and have experience working with veterans.

When selecting a veteran-friendly audiologist, look for the following:

  • Specialized training in audiology and hearing aid treatment.
  • Knowledge of VA benefits, including audiology services and hearing aids.
  • Comfort and understanding of working with veterans.
  • Experience with veterans' specific needs, including noise-induced hearing loss and tinnitus.

By working with a veteran-friendly audiologist, you can ensure that you receive the care and attention you need to improve your hearing health and access your VA benefits.
