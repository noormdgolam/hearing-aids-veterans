Finding Veteran-Friendly Audiologists in Colorado

By Dr. Sarah Mitchell, Audiologist & Veterans Advocate Updated: 2026-09-17
X f in

Find a Veteran-Friendly Audiologist in Colorado

As a veteran in Colorado, you're likely entitled to quality hearing healthcare services and benefits. With a comprehensive approach to hearing health, we've compiled a list of ways to find a veteran-friendly audiologist, maximizing your benefits and outcomes.

Eligibility for VA Benefits

  • Be a member of the U.S. Armed Forces, a veteran, or the spouse or dependent of a veteran.
  • Have served during a period of active duty or active duty for training, and meet certain criteria related to hearing loss or other medical conditions.
  • Be enrolled in the Department of Veterans Affairs (VA) healthcare system or have made arrangements to receive care at a VA medical facility.

VA Benefits and Services for Hearing Loss

The VA offers a variety of benefits and services to help veterans with hearing loss, including:

  • Comprehensive hearing evaluations and audiological assessments.
  • Treatment options, including the fitting of hearing aids and other assistive listening devices.
  • Compensation and pension benefits for hearing loss related to military service.
  • Access to a network of audiologists and hearing healthcare professionals trained to support veterans.

Finding a Veteran-Friendly Audiologist in Colorado

The VA recommends the following methods for finding a qualified audiologist:

  • Visit the VA website at va.gov and use the "Find a Doctor" tool to locate a VA-approved audiologist in Colorado.
  • Search online directories, such as the Academy of Doctors of Audiology (ADA) or the American Speech-Language-Hearing Association (ASHA), for audiologists who specialize in serving veterans.
  • Contact the Colorado Department of Veterans Affairs (CDVA) for recommendations on audiologists with expertise in serving veterans.

Verification of VA Benefits and Services

Before seeking care, it's essential to verify your eligibility for VA benefits and services. You can:

  • Contact your local VA Medical Center or Outpatient Clinic for guidance on enrollment and benefits.
  • Call the VA's toll-free phone number at 1-800-827-1000 to inquire about your eligibility and available services.
  • Create a personal account on the My HealtheVet portal to access your VA benefits and medical records.

What to Expect from a Veteran-Friendly Audiologist

A veteran-friendly audiologist should provide:

  • Personalized care with respect for your military service and unique needs.
  • Expertise in treating hearing loss related to military service, such as noise-induced hearing loss or tinnitus.
  • Coordinated care with other healthcare professionals, including primary care physicians and other specialists.
  • Clear explanations of your treatment options, including the benefits and limitations of hearing aids and other assistive listening devices.

By following these steps and finding a veteran-friendly audiologist, you can take advantage of your benefits and improve your hearing and overall well-being.
