Finding Veteran-Friendly Audiologists in Tennessee

By Dr. Sarah Mitchell, Audiologist & Veterans Advocate Updated: 2028-03-10

Supporting Tennessee's Veterans: Finding Audiologists Who Specialize in VA Benefits and Hearing Aids

Tennessee is home to a large number of veterans, many of whom have sacrificed their hearing health due to military service. If you're a veteran struggling with hearing loss or seeking VA benefits for hearing aids, locating an audiologist experienced in Veterans Affairs can be a daunting task.

This article serves as a comprehensive guide to finding veteran-friendly audiologists in Tennessee, helping you navigate the process of finding the right professional for your needs.

Eligibility for VA Benefits

The U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs (VA) offers a range of benefits, including hearing aids and other audiological services, to qualifying veterans. To be eligible, you typically need to have a service-connected disability rating or be enrolled in the VA Healthcare System.

Some common conditions that may qualify you for VA benefits, but not exhaustively, include:

  • Tinnitus (ringing in the ears)
  • sensorineural hearing loss
  • noise-induced hearing loss
  • Post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD) or other service-connected conditions that affect hearing

IHS Providers: Find an Audiologist Experienced in VA Benefits

The VA relies on a network of providers, known as VA-recognized IHS (Indian Health Service) facilities and non-federal community-based outpatient clinics (CBOCs), to deliver healthcare services, including audiology treatment. You can find a VA-recognized audiologist in Tennessee by:

  • Navigating the VA Facility Locator website and selecting 'Audiology & Speech Pathology' in the service dropdown.
  • Calling the VA's toll-free number at (877) 222-8387 to inquire about audiology services and benefits.

Additional Ways to Find a Veteran-Friendly Audiologist in Tennessee

While working through the VA's system can be helpful, you may also want to explore other means of finding an audiologist experienced in VA benefits:

  • Search online directories, such as the American Cochlear Implant Alliance (ACIA) or American Speech-Language-Hearing Association (ASHA), to locate professionals near you.
  • Asking for referrals from local VFW posts, veterans' groups, or healthcare organizations that serve veterans.

Preparing for Your Audiologist Appointment

Before your consultation, gather your relevant medical information, including:

  • A copy of your DD-214 (discharge records)
  • Veterans Identification Card
  • Audiological test results and any relevant documentation from the VA

Conclusion

Your hearing and overall well-being deserve the best possible care. By understanding your eligibility for VA benefits and taking advantage of resources to find a veteran-friendly audiologist, you can get the support you need to thrive in Tennessee.
