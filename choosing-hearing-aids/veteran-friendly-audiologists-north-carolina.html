Finding Veteran-Friendly Audiologists in North Carolina

By Dr. Sarah Mitchell, Audiologist & Veterans Advocate Updated: 2027-10-27

Find the Right Support for Your Hearing Needs in North Carolina

As a veteran in North Carolina, you may be entitled to receive hearing aids and other related services through the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs (VA). To ensure a seamless experience, it's crucial to find a qualified, veteran-friendly audiology professional who can help you navigate the VA benefits process and provide the care you need. Here are some tips to help you find the right audiologist in North Carolina:

  • Get a Referral from the VA: Start by contacting the VA hospital or medical facility nearest to you to inquire about a list of recommended audiologists. You can also reach out to your primary care physician or the Audiology and Speech Pathology Service within the VA.
  • Check with Your Insurance Provider: If you have private health insurance that covers hearing aid services, contact your insurance provider to ask about a list of in-network audiologists in North Carolina.
  • Verify Certification and Qualifications: When researching potential audiologists, ensure they hold a doctoral degree in audiology (Au.D.) or a similar degree, and are certified by the American Board of Audiology (ABA.) Additionally, check if they have experience in treating veteran populations.
  • Ambulatory Care Centers: The VA operates a network of Ambulatory Care Centers across North Carolina that offer audiology services. These centers can be an excellent entry point for veterans seeking comprehensive care. Find your nearest Ambulatory Care Center and ask about audiology services.
  • Look for Providers with Special Training: Some audiologists may have specialized knowledge and training in working with veterans, including those with Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD) or other conditions. Ask about their experience in this area when seeking care.

Some veteran-friendly audiologists to consider in North Carolina include:

  • Charlotte VA Medical Center**: The Charlotte VA Medical Center offers a comprehensive audiology program, including hearing testing, hearing aid fittings and adjustments, and cochlear implant services. Their staff is experienced in providing care for veterans with hearing loss and related conditions.
  • Audiology and Speech Pathology Clinic at the Fayetteville VA Medical Center**: This clinic provides a wide range of audiology services, including hearing testing and hearing aid evaluations. Their team members are certified audiologists with extensive experience working with veterans.

Once you've compiled a list of potential audiologists, don't hesitate to reach out to them with questions or concerns. When contacting the audiologist or their office, be sure to inquire about their experience working with veterans and their approach to helping those with hearing-related conditions. By doing your research and partnering with a qualified, veteran-friendly professional, you can take the first step towards improving your hearing and connecting with the care and support you deserve.

Additional Resources

If you're unsure where to start or need additional guidance on finding a qualified audiologist in North Carolina, consider reaching out to the following organizations:

  • U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs**: For general information on VA benefits and services, visit the VA website or call the VA toll-free phone number (1-800-827-1000).
  • American Academy of Audiology (AAA)**: For information on finding a qualified audiologist, visit the AAA website or search for a local audiologist via their online directory.
  • Veterans Service Organizations (VSOs)**: Local VSOs, such as the American Legion or the Disabled American Veterans (DAV), often provide resources and support for veterans navigating the VA benefits process. Contact your local VSO for assistance in finding a qualified audiologist.
