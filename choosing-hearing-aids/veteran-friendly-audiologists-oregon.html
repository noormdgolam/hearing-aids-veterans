Finding Veteran-Friendly Audiologists in Oregon

By Dr. Sarah Mitchell, Audiologist & Veterans Advocate Updated: 2027-12-26
X f in

Find a Veteran-Friendly Audiologist in Oregon

If you're a veteran looking for a hearing specialist in Oregon, you've come to the right place. Our state is home to many audiologists who have the training and experience to help you overcome hearing loss or other ear-related issues. In this guide, we'll walk you through the process of finding a veteran-friendly audiologist in Oregon, as well as explain how to access VA benefits to help cover the cost of hearing aids.

What to Look for in a Veteran-Friendly Audiologist

  • Experience Working with Veterans: Look for audiologists who have previous experience working with veterans, either in the VA system or through private practice. This experience is crucial in understanding the unique needs of veterans and providing the best possible care.
  • VA Benefits Expertise: Your audiologist should be knowledgeable about VA benefits and how to obtain them. They should be able to guide you through the process of applying for benefits and help you understand what you're eligible for.
  • Hearing Aid Coverage: Many veterans are eligible for VA benefits that cover the cost of hearing aids. Your audiologist should be aware of these benefits and help you access them.
  • State-of-the-Art Technology: Make sure your audiologist is up-to-date on the latest technology and equipment, including digital hearing aids and audiology software.

How to Find a Veteran-Friendly Audiologist in Oregon

To find a veteran-friendly audiologist in Oregon, follow these steps:

  • Check Online Directories: The American Speech-Language-Hearing Association (ASHA) and the American Academy of Audiology (AAA) both have directories of certified audiologists in Oregon. You can search by location, specialty, and other criteria to find a suitable audiologist.
  • Ask for Referrals: Ask friends, family members, or other veterans for recommendations. Word of mouth is often the best way to find a reliable audiologist.
  • Contact Your Local VA Office: Your local VA office can provide a list of audiologists who specialize in working with veterans. Many VA offices also offer referral services to help you find a qualified audiologist.
  • Check with Local Veterans Organizations: Local veterans organizations, such as the American Legion or VFW, often have lists of recommended audiologists who work well with veterans.

Accessing VA Benefits to Cover the Cost of Hearing Aids

If you're a veteran eligible for VA benefits, you may be able to cover the cost of hearing aids through the VA system. To access these benefits, follow these steps:

  • Contact Your Local VA Office: Reach out to your local VA office to inquire about your eligibility for VA benefits. They will guide you through the application process and explain what benefits you're eligible for.
  • Apply for VA Benefits: Submit your application for VA benefits, which may include documentation of your hearing loss and any medical treatment you've received.
  • Visit an Eligible Audiologist: Once your application is approved, you can visit an eligible audiologist to receive a hearing examination and discuss your options for hearing aids.
