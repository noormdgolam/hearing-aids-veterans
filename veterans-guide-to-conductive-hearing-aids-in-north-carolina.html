Veterans Guide to Conductive Hearing Aids in North Carolina

Published: July 15, 2026 • Written by Editorial Team

As a veteran in North Carolina, you may be eligible for financial assistance to help cover the costs of conductive hearing aids through various programs and services. In this guide, we will cover the key aspects of conductive hearing aids, how to access them, and available resources to help you make informed decisions about your hearing health.

What are Conductive Hearing Aids?

Conductive hearing aids are designed to help individuals with middle ear hearing loss, which is typically caused by damage to the eardrum or other components of the middle ear. They work by amplifying sound and helping to bypass the damaged middle ear components, allowing the sound to reach the inner ear and the brain.

There are several types of conductive hearing aids available, including:

  • Bilateral cochlear implants, which are surgically implanted devices that bypass damaged ear structures to directly stimulate the auditory nerve
  • Conventional hearing aids with external sound processors and ear molds
  • Bone-anchored hearing aids (BAHAs), which use a surgically implanted device to directly transmit sound to the inner ear
  • Hearables, which include cochlear implants and BAHAs that are designed for more precise implantation in the ear

Accessing Conductive Hearing Aids in North Carolina

To access conductive hearing aids in North Carolina, you may need to follow these steps:

  1. Contact your primary care physician or an ear, nose, and throat (ENT) specialist to discuss your hearing loss and determine the best course of treatment
  2. Consult with a hearing specialist or audiologist to determine the most effective type of conductive hearing aid for your specific needs
  3. Apply for financial assistance through the Department of Veterans Affairs (VA) or private insurance providers, if applicable
  4. Visit your local VA medical center or an approved private vendor to obtain and fit your conductive hearing aid

Available Resources in North Carolina

As a veteran in North Carolina, you may be eligible for financial assistance to help cover the costs of conductive hearing aids through the following resources:

  • The VA's Hearing Aid Program, which provides eligible veterans with free or reduced-cost hearing aids, including conductive hearing aids
  • The North Carolina Division of Services for the Deaf and Hard of Hearing (NCDHHS-DDHH), which offers assistive listening devices and hearing aid maintenance services
  • The North Carolina Veterans Affairs office, which provides financial assistance and support for veterans with hearing loss

Conclusion

As a veteran in North Carolina, you have access to a range of resources and services to help you navigate the process of obtaining conductive hearing aids. By understanding the different types of conductive hearing aids available and following the steps outlined in this guide, you can make informed decisions about your hearing health and access the financial assistance you need to improve your quality of life.
